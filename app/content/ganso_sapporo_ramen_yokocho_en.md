Ganso Sapporo Ramen Yokocho (元祖さっぽろラーメン横丁) is a short covered alley in **Susukino**, Sapporo’s main nightlife district. The name **Ganso** (“original”) marks it as one of the city’s early post-war ramen lanes — a cluster of tiny counters rather than a single brand. You are not booking one restaurant: you walk the passage, compare menu boards, and sit at whichever stall fits your mood and queue tolerance.

**What you are eating:** Most stalls here focus on **Sapporo miso ramen**. The style is heavier than Tokyo shoyu: miso tare is often stir-fried in a wok before it meets pork-and-chicken broth, then topped with **thick curly noodles**, chashu, scallions, and — very often — **butter and sweet corn**. Spicy miso (*karami-miso*) is common if you want more heat. Bowls typically run **¥900–¥1,200**; sizes and toppings vary by stall.

**How to visit:** Seating is almost always **counter-only**, so expect a short wait at dinner and after 10 p.m., especially on winter weekends when Susukino is packed. There is no single “house bowl” for the whole alley — each shop sets its own hours, holidays, and ticket-machine flow. Read the board outside, order at that stall’s machine or counter, and eat where you ordered. If one line is long, walk to the next door; that comparison is the point of the yokocho.

**Choosing a stall:** Treat the alley as a sampler lane, not a ranked list. Long-running counters (names change over the years) each tweak broth richness, noodle firmness, and miso sweetness differently. A practical approach: pick the shortest reasonable queue, order the shop’s basic miso, then try another stall on a second night if you are staying nearby. Use the **Maps link above** for each pin — hours are **per shop**, not alley-wide.

**Area & pairing:** Susukino is walkable from central Sapporo stations, with hotels, bars, and late-night food all around. The alley works well after skiing or a long flight when you want something warm and fast. Daytime you can combine it with Odori Park or Sapporo Station; for a broader miso comparison in the city, pair this stop with dedicated single-shop guides such as [Ramen Shingen](/ramen/ramen_shingen_en) or [Sumire Sapporo](/ramen/sumire_sapporo_en) on another evening.
