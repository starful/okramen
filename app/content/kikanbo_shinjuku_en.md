## Overview
Kikanbo Shinjuku offers a unique **Onimiso Ramen** experience in Tokyo's Shinjuku district. This acclaimed shop is famous for its rich miso broth with customizable levels of *karamiso* (spicy chili) and *shibi* (Sichuan peppercorn numbing). It's a must-visit for travelers craving bold flavors and is very **solo-friendly** with its efficient counter seating. For trip planning, always verify current operating hours and temporary closures on Google Maps. This guide provides practical advice, not official shop information.

## What to order
Focus your first visit on Kikanbo's signature **Onimiso Ramen**. A key part is customizing your spice (*karamiso*) and numbing (*shibi*) levels at the ticket machine.

For **Karamiso (Spicy)**, options typically include:
*   **Standard (普通 - futsuu):** Mild kick.
*   **Medium (辛め - karame):** Noticeable heat.
*   **Hot (鬼辛 - onikara):** Genuinely spicy.

For **Shibi (Numbing)**:
*   **Standard (普通 - futsuu):** Subtle tingle.
*   **Medium (痺れ - shibire):** Pronounced numbing.
*   **Hot (鬼痺れ - onishibire):** Intense numbing.

**First-timers should begin with Standard (普通) for both spice and numbing.** This allows you to savor the unique flavors without being overwhelmed. The "Oni" (demon) levels are exceptionally intense, for high tolerance only.

Common add-ons on the ticket machine:
*   **Extra Chashu (チャーシュー - chaashuu):** Braised pork belly.
*   **Extra Noodles (麺大盛り - men oomori):** For a larger meal.
*   **Ajitama (味玉 - ajitama):** Seasoned soft-boiled egg.

Ramen bowls usually cost **¥1,000–¥1,300**. Kikanbo, like most ramen shops, uses a **ticket machine (食券機 - shokkenki)** for ordering. Select items, insert **cash (yen bills/coins are essential; most machines are cash-only)**, and receive a ticket to hand to staff upon seating.

## Visit tips
**Getting There:** Kikanbo Shinjuku is easily accessible.
*   From **Shinjuku Station (JR, Metro)**: 10-15 minute walk.
*   From **Shinjuku-sanchome Station (Metro, Toei)**: 5-7 minute walk.
*   From **Seibu-Shinjuku Station (Seibu Line)**: ~5 minute walk.
Always use Google Maps for precise walking directions ("Kikanbo Shinjuku").

**Queue & Ordering:** Expect queues, especially during peak times:
*   **Lunch:** 12:00 PM - 1:30 PM
*   **Dinner:** 6:00 PM - 8:30 PM
Arriving before 11:30 AM or after 9:00 PM can reduce wait times. Observe if others buy tickets before or after joining the line.

Navigating the **ticket machine** without Japanese:
*   Look for **photos** and **prices (¥)**.
*   Keywords: ラーメン (ramen), 味噌 (miso), チャーシュー (chaashuu), 味玉 (ajitama), 麺大盛り (men oomori).
Insert cash and collect your ticket. If unsure, point to a picture or politely ask staff for help. **Cash is required.**

**Solo Friendly:** Dining alone is common and comfortable here; counter seating is designed for individual, efficient meals.

**Nearby Alternatives & Area:** Shinjuku offers much to explore:
*   **Shinjuku Gyoen National Garden:** A tranquil escape.
*   **Tokyo Metropolitan Government Building:** Free panoramic city views.
*   **Kabukicho & Golden Gai:** Explore entertainment districts in the evening.
For other ramen styles, consider **Taiyou NO Tomatomen** (tomato-based) or **Tantanmen Sandaime** (spicy tantanmen). Always check Google Maps for current hours, last orders, and holiday schedules for all establishments.
