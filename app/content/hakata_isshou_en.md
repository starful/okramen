## Overview

Hakata Isshou, at 3-1-6 Hakataekihigashi, Hakata-ku, Fukuoka, is a 'Local Gem' for authentic Hakata-style tonkotsu ramen. It offers Fukuoka's culinary heart: rich, milky, intensely savory pork bone broth with thin, firm noodles. A local favorite, it provides a quality, traditional ramen experience focused solely on superb broth and noodles, not tourist frills. Expect a focused environment where quality takes center stage.

## What to Order

Ordering at Hakata Isshou is straightforward, even with a language barrier.

**First-Time Order: The Signature Bowl**
For your first visit, order the shop’s standard ramen. This highlights their tonkotsu broth's pure flavor and the noodles' perfect texture. Prices typically range ¥800–¥1,100 for a regular bowl.

**Using the Ticket Machine (食券機, *shokkenki*)**
Like many shops, Hakata Isshou may use a ticket machine for ordering. Often near the entrance and cash-only, so have yen ready. Look for:
- **ラーメン (Ramen):** The standard bowl.
- **チャーシューメン (Chāshū-men):** Ramen with extra braised pork (chashu).
- **替え玉 (Kaedama):** An extra serving of noodles, ideal if you finish your initial noodles but want more.
Pictures guide choices. If unsure, point to a simple ramen picture.

**Customization & Condiments**
After the basic bowl, consider adding extra chashu on a return visit. Request a *kaedama* from staff after your first noodle portion – it's a Hakata tradition. Tables feature condiments like pickled ginger (*beni shoga*), spicy pickled mustard greens (*karashi takana*), and sesame. Add to taste after appreciating the original broth.

## Visiting Hakata Isshou

For a smooth visit, here are key tips.

**Queues and Peak Times**
Queues are common at this popular local spot.
- **Lunch Peak:** 12:00 PM – 1:00 PM
- **Dinner Peak:** 6:00 PM – 8:00 PM
Visit outside these hours (e.g., before noon or after 8:00 PM) to reduce wait times. Follow local queuing etiquette: wait patiently, don't block pathways, and be ready to order.

**Confirm Operating Hours**
Hours, holidays, and last order times change. **Always verify current info via Google Maps** on visit day. Screenshotting the Google Maps listing helps if internet is unreliable. Don't rely on third-party guides for real-time details. Expect a focused, quick-service environment, not extensive English assistance.

**Ramen Etiquette**
Ramen shops are compact for quick meals. Eat hot. Slurping is acceptable and shows enjoyment. Once finished, vacate promptly for the next customer.
