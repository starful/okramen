Hello, readers! This is your 'JinjaMap' editor, here to add depth to your travels.

Today, I will guide you to **'Hokkaido Jingu (北海道神宮)'**, an essential stop on any trip to Sapporo, the heart of the snowy land of Hokkaido. Just a 15-minute subway ride from central Sapporo, this shrine is surrounded by a dense primeval forest that makes you forget you're in a city.

Established during the Meiji era to pioneer the then-unknown land of Hokkaido, this place still holds the fervent aspirations and hopes of the people from 150 years ago.
Why not take a moment to pause your journey here, enjoying a warm grilled rice cake amidst beautiful scenery—cherry blossoms in spring, autumn leaves, and pure white snowscapes in winter?

---

### 1. 🙏 Deities and Origins: A Sanctuary for the Gods Who Pioneered Hokkaido

Hokkaido Jingu was born along with the beginning of this land.

#### **【Enshrined Deities (御祭神): The Three Pioneer Gods and Emperor Meiji】**

This shrine is dedicated to the **'Three Pioneer Deities'**: **Okunitama-no-kami (god of the land)**, **Ōkuninushi-no-kami (god of nation-building)**, and **Sukunahikona-no-kami (god of medicine and wisdom)**. They are the gods who were enshrined to develop the harsh land of Hokkaido and protect its people.
Later, **Emperor Meiji**, who decreed the development of Hokkaido, was also enshrined here, making them powerful guardian deities who watch over all aspects of life, including **national peace**, **business prosperity**, **health and longevity**, and **traffic safety**.

#### **【Origins and History: From Ezochi to Hokkaido】**

In 1869, Emperor Meiji renamed 'Ezochi' to 'Hokkaido' and initiated its development. To soothe the hearts of the settlers who had to battle the harsh nature, a shrine was established at its current location in 1871.
At that time, it was called 'Sapporo Jinja' (Sapporo Shrine), but in recognition of its status, it was promoted to **'Jingu' (Grand Shrine)** in 1964. For the people of Hokkaido, it is like a spiritual home.

### 2. 🌲 A Stroll Through the Precincts: Healing and Squirrels in the Primeval Forest

The shrine grounds, connected to Maruyama Park, are nature itself. Here are some points you shouldn't miss.

#### **The Second Torii Gate and the Main Approach**

The **Second Torii**, which serves as the main gate of the shrine, boasts a magnificent presence. As you walk along the straight path past it, the fresh air from the towering trees on both sides will clear your head. If you're lucky, you might even spot a cute **Ezo Risu (Hokkaido squirrel)** scurrying between the trees!

#### **Kaitaku Jinja (開拓神社 - Pioneer Shrine)**

This is a small shrine located next to the main hall. It enshrines 37 individuals who made significant contributions to the development of Hokkaido, such as Mamiya Rinzō and Inō Tadataka. It's a good place to reflect and show gratitude for the hard work of the ancestors who cultivated this barren land.

#### **Rokkatei Jingu Chaya (Hangan-sama)**

The highlight of your visit! The famous Hokkaido confectionery **'Rokkatei'** operates a tea house within the shrine grounds. Its specialty, **'Hangan-sama'**, is a grilled buckwheat mochi (rice cake) filled with red bean paste. It can only be tasted here, and it's absolutely delicious when enjoyed with the complimentary hot tea. (The price is very reasonable too!)

### 3. 📜 Goshuin and Souvenirs: Where Hello Kitty Meets Cherry Blossoms

The souvenirs at Hokkaido Jingu are cute and special.

#### **【Goshuin (御朱印)】**

In addition to the Goshuin with the elegant calligraphy of **'北海道神宮'** (Hokkaido Jingu), you can also receive a separate Goshuin from the **'Kaitaku Jinja'** (Pioneer Shrine) on the grounds. It's fun to collect both.

#### **【Omamori (お守り) & Goods】**

- **Hello Kitty Charm**: A charm featuring Hello Kitty dressed as a shrine maiden is very popular with children and women.
- **Rilakkuma Ema**: Write your wishes on a cute Rilakkuma ema (wooden wishing plaque) and hang it up.
- **Cherry Blossom Bell Charm**: As a famous spot for cherry blossoms, the beautiful cherry blossom-shaped bell charm is also a popular item.

### 4. 🏙️ Nearby Attractions: Sapporo's Nature and Cuisine

After visiting the shrine, delve deeper into the charms of Sapporo.

#### **1. Maruyama Zoo**

- **Location**: A 10-minute walk from the shrine.
- **Features**: This is Hokkaido's premier zoo. The underwater tunnel where you can watch polar bears swim is famous. Highly recommended for family travelers!

#### **2. Morihico (Cafe)**

- **Location**: Near Maruyama Park.
- **Features**: A sacred spot for Sapporo's cafe scene. It's a renovated old wooden house covered in ivy, serving excellent rich hand-drip coffee and cake. It's especially romantic in the winter.

#### **3. Odori Park & TV Tower**

- **Access**: Two subway stops away.
- **Features**: The heart of Sapporo and the main venue for the Snow Festival. End your day by enjoying the night view of Sapporo from the TV Tower's observation deck.

### ♨️ Relax at a Nearby Onsen: Jozankei Onsen (定山渓温泉)

Just a scenic one-hour bus ride from Sapporo, Jozankei Onsen is the perfect *day-trip onsen* escape after exploring the city. Nestled in a picturesque valley along the Toyohira River, its sodium chloride springs are renowned for their warming and *relaxing* properties, ideal for soothing tired muscles. With numerous hotels offering *day-trip onsen* plans, it's incredibly convenient to immerse yourself in the natural beauty and therapeutic waters. It offers a tranquil retreat *nearby The Heart of Hokkaido: Discovering Pioneer History and Healing at Sapporo's Hokkaido Jingu Shrine*, making it an ideal post-shrine rejuvenation spot.
