Hello, this is the editor of 'JinjaMap,' on a journey to find hidden gems and sacred sites around the world.

Today, I will guide you to Sendai (仙台), the city of trees and the heart of Japan's Tohoku region. Did you know that nestled within its lush green forests lies a magnificent shrine that seems to have traveled back in time? The star of our show is **Osaki Hachimangu (大崎八幡宮)**, a national treasure that embodies the ambition and aesthetic sense of the Warring States hero, Date Masamune.

With its brilliant gold leaf decorations blooming over black lacquer, the powerful aura of prayers for victory from warriors 400 years ago, and even Sendai's delicious local cuisine, this is more than just a tourist spot. If you want a special journey that steps into history, let's head to Osaki Hachimangu together now.

### 1. 🙏 Deities and Origins: The God of Victory and Date Masamune's Masterpiece

Before you admire the shrine's grand appearance, understanding the stories of the gods enshrined here and its origins will double your appreciation. Osaki Hachimangu is where the god of victory met the determination of a hero.

#### **【Enshrined Deities (御祭神): Guardians of Victory and Peace】**

The principal deity here is the 15th emperor, **Emperor Ojin (応神天皇)**.
Known since ancient times as the "god of the bow and arrow," he was absolutely revered by samurai warriors. He possesses a powerful force that governs not only victory in war but all of life's competitions. That is why for modern people, it is known as the ultimate power spot for **prayers for certain victory (必勝祈願)**, **success**, and **passing exams**.

Additionally, his parents, Emperor Chuai and Empress Jingu, are enshrined alongside him, giving the shrine a benevolent aspect that protects family peace and children's health. It is, in a sense, a home for deities where strength and warmth coexist.

#### **【Origin and History: The Aesthetics of Date Masamune】**

The true value of Osaki Hachimangu lies in its origin. **Date Masamune**, the first lord of the Sendai domain and known as the "One-Eyed Dragon," decided to make Sendai his eternal base and believed a powerful guardian shrine was necessary to protect the city and its people.

He was not satisfied with merely building a shrine. In 1607, he summoned the best artisans of the era from Kyoto and Osaka, pouring the era's finest architectural technology and artistry into completing this shrine. Thus, a masterpiece of the **"Momoyama style"**, featuring brilliant gold leaf and richly colored carvings over a black lacquer base, was born.

This shrine was built to the northwest of Sendai Castle, in the "kimon (鬼門)" direction, from which demons were believed to enter. This was a calculated placement by Masamune, intended to block evil spirits with the shrine's sacred power. Even after 400 years, Osaki Hachimangu stands proudly as the guardian deity of Sendai.

### 2. 🌲 A Stroll Through the Grounds: The Majesty of the Black Pavilion and the Serenity of the Forest

The moment you step into the grounds, the noise of the city vanishes, replaced by a sacred silence.

#### **The Forest Path and Nagatoko (長床)**

After passing through the giant torii gate, a path lined with cedar trees soaring into the sky unfolds. Walking while breathing in the fresh air calms the mind and prepares you to meet the gods. At the top of the steep stone steps, the **"Nagatoko (gate)"**, a National Important Cultural Property, awaits. It is a simple wooden structure, but its sturdiness exudes the strength it has gathered over many years.

#### **The National Treasure (國寶) Main Hall: A Harmony of Black and Gold**

Passing through the Nagatoko, the main hall and worship hall, designated as a **National Treasure**, finally reveal themselves.
The gleaming gold leaf decorations shining out from the deep darkness of the black lacquer, and the ornate carvings of dragons and phoenixes under the eaves, are overwhelming to behold. This is the essence of **"Momoyama architecture,"** which represents the splendor of Toyotomi Hideyoshi's era. It is said to have been a model for Nikko Toshogu. Take a moment to stand before this building and feel the breath of the artisans from 400 years ago.

- **Worship Tip**: At the worship hall, offer your prayers reverently with the "ni-rei, ni-hakushu, ichi-rei (two bows, two claps, one bow)" method and receive the energy of certain victory.

### 3. 📜 Goshuin and Talismans: Holding a Charm of Victory

Here are some of the special items you can take with you, filled with the unique energy of Osaki Hachimangu.

#### **【Goshuin (御朱印)】**

The goshuin here is simple yet full of dignity. The bold calligraphy of **'大崎八幡宮'** written in the center and the vivid red shrine seal make for a wonderful souvenir and charm in itself.

#### **【Omamori (お守り) & Engimono】**

- **Victory Charm (勝守, Katsumori)**: A charm with the character '勝' (victory) embroidered in gold on a black background. With its powerful design, as if imbued with Masamune's spirit, it's the perfect gift for students, job seekers, or anyone facing an important project.
- **Matsukawa Daruma**: A blue Daruma doll, a traditional craft of Sendai. This Daruma, with its wide, bulging eyes, is said to watch over all directions and ward off evil spirits.

### 4. 🥢 Nearby Attractions: The Flavors and History of Sendai

After visiting the shrine, enjoy the history and culinary delights of Sendai.

#### **1. Sendai Castle Ruins (Aoba Castle Ruins)**

- **Access**: About 15 minutes via the "Loople Sendai" sightseeing bus.
- **Features**: A landmark where the equestrian statue of Date Masamune overlooks the city. It's the best viewpoint, offering a panoramic view of Sendai city and the Pacific Ocean.

#### **2. Gyutan (Beef Tongue) Restaurant 'Rikyu'**

- **Features**: You can't say you've been to Sendai without trying its soul food, **grilled gyutan**. The thick, chewy texture and charcoal aroma are superb. There are many branches near the shrine or around Sendai Station, making it easy to find.

#### **3. Zunda Saryo (Zunda Shake)**

- **Features**: "Zunda," made from ground edamame, is a Sendai specialty. The **Zunda Shake**, in particular, washes away travel fatigue with its sweet and savory flavor. Be sure to try it at Sendai Station!
