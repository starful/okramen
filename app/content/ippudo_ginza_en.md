## Overview

Ippudo Ginza offers a highly regarded Tonkotsu ramen experience in the heart of Tokyo's upscale Ginza district. Located at **4-10-3 Ginza, Chuo City, Tokyo 104-0061, Japan**, this 'Premium' category branch is part of a globally recognized chain, known for its consistent quality. Tonkotsu, meaning "pork bone," refers to the rich, milky, and savory broth slow-cooked from pork bones. It provides an excellent introduction to this deeply flavorful ramen style for travelers. While Ippudo maintains high standards, always verify the specific operating hours and menu for *this* Ginza location via Google Maps, as slight variations can occur between branches.

## What to Order

At Ippudo Ginza, your choice centers on their acclaimed Tonkotsu ramen, typically highlighting two signature bowls:

*   **Shiromaru Motoaji:** The classic Hakata-style Tonkotsu. This bowl features a traditional, milder pork broth, thin straight noodles, and standard toppings like chashu, kikurage, and green onions. Ideal for a pure, straightforward Tonkotsu flavor.
*   **Akamaru Shinaji:** Ippudo's 'modern' interpretation. It builds on the Shiromaru broth with a blended miso paste and fragrant garlic oil, adding layers of depth and umami.

A standard bowl generally costs between **¥800 and ¥1,100**. You can customize your order with popular add-ons such as a seasoned soft-boiled egg (ajitama), extra chashu slices, or a "kaedama" (an additional serving of noodles). Most ramen shops, including Ippudo, use a ticket vending machine: insert cash, select items, and hand the ticket to staff.
