## Overview
Rokurinsha Haneda provides an essential Tokyo ramen experience for travelers, conveniently located within Haneda Airport Terminal 3. Specializing in *tsukemen*, this shop serves thick, chewy noodles separately from an intensely rich, concentrated dipping broth. This allows diners to control the flavor intensity with each bite. Rokurinsha is celebrated for its deep umami, substantial portions, and consistent quality, making it an excellent choice for a hearty meal before or after your flight. It's a popular spot for both locals and international visitors.

## What to Order
For your first visit, the **Tokusei Tsukemen** (Special Tsukemen) is highly recommended. This comprehensive set typically includes a generous portion of noodles, the signature concentrated broth, and toppings like a perfectly cooked `ajitamago` (marinated egg) and tender `chashu` (sliced pork). The broth is a powerful, long-simmered blend of pork and seafood, known for its viscous texture and profound umami.

A standard bowl will cost around ¥1,000–¥1,300. `Kaedama` (extra noodles) might be available; check the ticket machine options.

After finishing your noodles, enhance your experience with **soup-wari**. Ask the staff (holding out your bowl and saying "soup-wari kudasai") to dilute your remaining concentrated broth with a lighter stock, transforming it into a delicious, drinkable soup to conclude your meal.

## Visit Tips
*   **Queueing:** Rokurinsha is very popular. Expect queues, especially during peak meal times (lunch/dinner) and busy airport periods. Service is efficient, but patience is key.
*   **Ticket Machine:** Order via a vending machine at the entrance. While some have English, many are Japanese-only. Look for pictures of the "Tokusei Tsukemen" or the top-left button (often the recommended item). Prices in ¥ are clear. Insert cash first (¥1,000, ¥5,000, ¥10,000 notes and coins), then select. Hand tickets to staff upon seating. Credit cards are typically not accepted at the machine.
*   **Eating Promptly:** Tsukemen noodles are best eaten firm. Begin your meal soon after it's served to enjoy the ideal texture.
*   **Check Hours:** Always verify operating hours, last order times, and any holiday closures via Google Maps or the Haneda Airport official website before your visit. Screenshot this information for offline access.

## How to Get There
Rokurinsha Haneda is located in **Terminal 3 (International Terminal) of Haneda Airport**, within the "Edo Koji" dining area.

*   **From within Terminal 3:** Follow signs for "Edo Koji" or "Restaurant Area." The shop is usually well-marked.
*   **From Terminals 1 or 2:** Take the free inter-terminal shuttle bus or the Keikyu Line/Tokyo Monorail to Terminal 3 Station. Rokurinsha is a short walk from the station exit inside the terminal.
*   **From Central Tokyo:** Take the Keikyu Line or Tokyo Monorail directly to Haneda Airport Terminal 3 Station.

## Nearby Alternatives and Further Exploration
If you're seeking to explore beyond Rokurinsha's tsukemen, Tokyo offers a vast ramen landscape. Consider these acclaimed shops for different styles:

*   **Hakata Ippudo Roppongi:** Dive into rich, creamy Hakata-style tonkotsu (pork bone) ramen, known for thin noodles and deep flavor. [Hakata Ippudo Roppongi](/ramen/hakata_ippudo_roppongi_en).
*   **Hirugao Tokyo:** For a lighter contrast, Hirugao specializes in refined `shio` (salt-based) ramen, offering a clear, delicate, yet flavorful broth. [Hirugao Tokyo](/ramen/hirugao_tokyo_en).

These options are for broadening your ramen experience across Tokyo, rather than being immediate alternatives within Ota.
